Renovation contracts often include debris removal in theory and exclude it in practice. Understanding what your contractor is — and is not — responsible for helps you budget correctly and avoids a post-project dispute.

## What Contractors Typically Remove

Most contractors haul away their own materials: cut lumber, drywall scraps, packaging. This is standard practice and should be confirmed in writing.

## What Often Gets Left

Old cabinetry, appliances, flooring, windows, doors, and demo debris frequently end up stacked in garages or driveways — especially when contractors lack hauling capacity or charge extra for it.

## Sorting Before Removal

Before calling for a removal, sort the pile: salvageable materials (intact cabinetry, working appliances) can be donated. Recyclables (metal, clean wood, concrete) should be separated.
