Almost every homeowner has tried and failed to clean out their garage at least twice. It always starts with good intentions and ends with everything shoved back in because the sun went down. Here is a system that actually works.

## Why Most Garage Cleanouts Fail Before They Start

The problem isn't motivation. It's a vision. You walk into your garage, see a decade of accumulated stuff, and start moving everything at once and decide whether to keep, toss, donate, or sell. Within two hours, decision fatigue sets in, and everything goes back where it was or into one of the corners you just cleaned and emptied.

The solution is simple: separate the physical work from the decision work. Do not try to do both at the same time.

## Step 1: Empty First, Sort Second

Get everything out of the garage and onto the driveway. No sorting yet. Just get everything out in the open. You achieve two things with this: it gives you a clear picture of what you actually have, and it forces you to commit to the process. You cannot leave it all on the driveway overnight.

## Step 2: Three Sections, Not Five

Keep it simple. Three physical areas on your driveway:

- **Keep:** things you use regularly or genuinely need
- **Gone:** trash, broken items, things that can be donated or recycled
- **Unsure:** anything you hesitate on goes here, not in "keep"

At the end, go through the "unsure" pile once. If you still hesitate, it goes in "gone."

## Step 3: Deep Clean Before Anything Goes Back In

With the garage empty, this is your opportunity to properly clean the floor, walls, and shelves. Sweep, vacuum, and mop. Wipe down any shelving units. This takes about two hours, and it is the part most people skip and then regret.

## Step 4: Only What Earns Its Place Comes Back In

Everything that goes back in the garage should have a designated spot. Group by category such as tools, sports gear, seasonal items, and car supplies.
