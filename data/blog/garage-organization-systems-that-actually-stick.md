A freshly cleaned garage feels transformative — but within six months, many return to their previous state. The culprit is rarely laziness; it's almost always an organization system that doesn't match how people actually use the space.

## Zone-Based Organization

Assign explicit zones to recurring activities: lawn care, sports equipment, tools, seasonal storage. Items migrate back to their zone naturally because the zone makes sense.

## Vertical Storage Is Non-Negotiable

Wall-mounted pegboards, slotted track systems, and ceiling-mounted racks reclaim enormous floor space. Heavy items go low; light, infrequently accessed items go high.

## Clear Bins, Labeled Precisely

Opaque bins become mystery boxes. Clear bins with specific labels (not "miscellaneous") enable anyone in the household to find and return items.

## Leave Slack Space Intentionally

Systems that fill every inch fail because real life generates new items. Design for 70–80% capacity and leave designated open space for additions.

## The Entry Point Discipline

The most critical habit: nothing enters the garage without an assigned home. If a new item arrives and has no zone, add it deliberately or reconsider whether it belongs there.
