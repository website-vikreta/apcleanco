Few things derail a rental turnaround like a tenant who leaves half their belongings behind. Understanding the right approach — legally and operationally — saves time and avoids liability.

## Know Your Local Abandonment Laws

Most jurisdictions require a waiting period (often 15–30 days) and a formal written notice before a landlord can legally dispose of abandoned property. Consult a local attorney if quantities are significant.

## Document Everything With Photos

Before removing a single item, photograph all rooms and every item left behind. This creates a timestamped record that protects you from future claims.

## Separate Valuable From Junk

Furniture in good condition may be claimable or resellable. True junk — broken appliances, rotted materials, bulk trash — should be removed quickly to avoid pest and health issues.
